How EMDR Helps Reprocess Fear Triggers

By pairing brief attention to feared images or sensations with bilateral stimulation, EMDR can reduce the emotional charge linked to triggers. The process aims to integrate new, more balanced information so responses feel safer. Skills for grounding and regulation are woven in to support progress.

EMDR for Fear and Phobias in Singapore: How It Works

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR) is a structured therapy that helps the brain process distressing memories and cues that fuel fear and phobias. Using guided eye movements or other bilateral stimulation, you revisit difficult moments safely and reduce their emotional intensity. Many clients notice that previously overwhelming triggers feel more manageable in daily life. In Singapore, EMDR is used to address phobias that interfere with work, commuting, and family routines.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is EMDR therapy and how does it help with fear and phobias?

EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) is an evidence-based therapy that uses bilateral stimulation (eye movements, taps, or tones) to help your brain reprocess fear memories and threat cues. For phobias, it targets the first/worst memory and feared images/sensations, reducing the emotional charge so triggers feel manageable without prolonged exposure. It can be used alone or alongside CBT/exposure. It’s generally safe; you stay in control, though temporary increases in emotion or vivid dreams can occur.

How many EMDR sessions will I need for a phobia, and what happens in a session?

Many specific phobias improve in about 3–6 EMDR sessions; complex or trauma-related fears may take 8–12+ sessions. Sessions last 60–90 minutes and follow an eight-phase protocol: history and plan, calming skills, targeting fear memories/triggers, sets of bilateral stimulation with brief check-ins, installing positive beliefs, body scan, and closure. You don’t have to describe every detail; the therapist paces the work and ensures stabilisation between sessions.

Is EMDR available in Singapore, and what about cost and coverage?

Yes. Look for EMDR-trained clinicians registered with bodies such as the Singapore Association for Counselling (SAC), the Singapore Register of Psychologists (SRP), or the Singapore Medical Council (for psychiatrists). Private fees commonly range from about SGD 150–300+ per 60 minutes (longer sessions may cost more). Some employer benefits or Integrated Shield Plans may reimburse psychotherapy with the right referral/diagnosis; MediSave generally does not cover private outpatient psychotherapy. Telehealth EMDR is available in Singapore, though in-person may be preferable for high-distress cases.
